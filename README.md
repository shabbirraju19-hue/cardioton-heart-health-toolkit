## Understanding Blood Pressure

### What is Blood Pressure?

Blood pressure is the force of blood pushing against the walls of your arteries as your heart pumps blood through your body. It's measured in millimeters of mercury (mmHg) and recorded as two numbers:

- **Systolic Pressure (top number):** The pressure when your heart beats and pumps blood out
- **Diastolic Pressure (bottom number):** The pressure when your heart rests between beats

### Why Blood Pressure Matters

Healthy blood pressure is essential for proper organ function. When BP is too high (hypertension), it puts strain on your arteries, heart, and other organs. Over time, uncontrolled hypertension can lead to:

- Heart attack and heart failure
- Stroke
- Kidney disease
- Vision loss
- Cognitive decline

### Risk Factors for High Blood Pressure

**Modifiable Risk Factors:**
- Unhealthy diet (high sodium, low potassium)
- Physical inactivity
- Obesity
- Excessive alcohol consumption
- Tobacco use
- Chronic stress
- Poor sleep quality

**Non-Modifiable Risk Factors:**
- Family history of hypertension
- Age (risk increases with age)
- Ethnicity (higher risk in South Asian populations)
- Gender (men at higher risk until age 64)

### Prevention and Management

**Dietary Approaches:**
- Follow the DASH diet (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension)
- Reduce sodium to less than 2,300mg per day
- Increase potassium-rich foods (bananas, spinach, sweet potatoes)
- Limit processed and restaurant foods

**Physical Activity:**
- Aim for 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week
- Include strength training twice weekly
- Incorporate daily movement and reduce sitting time

**Stress Management:**
- Practice meditation or deep breathing
- Engage in hobbies and social activities
- Ensure adequate sleep (7-9 hours)
- Consider yoga or tai chi

### Monitoring Your BP

Regular monitoring is crucial for managing blood pressure:

- **Home Monitoring:** Check BP at the same time daily
- **Proper Technique:** Sit quietly for 5 minutes, arm at heart level
- **Record Keeping:** Track readings to identify patterns
- **Use Our Calculator:** Assess your risk category instantly
